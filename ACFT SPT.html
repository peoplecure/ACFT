In SPT event of the ACFT, the personnel throws a 10-pound medicine ball backward and overhead for distance. The SPT event represents tasks requiring quick explosive movements to maneuver equipment and personnel.
Use the provided towels or rags to remove excess moisture or debris from the medicine ball.
The Soldiers will face away from the start line, grasp the medicine ball (10 pounds) with both hands at hip level and stand with both heels at (but not on or over) the start line. Grasp the ball firmly and as far around the sides of the ball as possible.
As directed by the grader, the Soldier in lane one executes throw one. Soldiers are permitted several preparatory movements flexing at the trunk, knees, and hips while lowering the ball between their legs.
When directed by the grader, the Soldier in lane two executes throw one.
Throw one is a practice throw, so it is NOT recorded
Soldiers will have two record attempts on the Standing Power Throw. Soldiers in lanes one and two will alternately execute record throw one and two. Soldiers are permitted several preparatory movements flexing at the trunk, knees, and hips while lowering the ball between their legs.
A record attempt will not count if a Solder throws a fault throw. A fault throw means that a Soldier steps on or beyond the start line or falls to the ground.
Two fault throws count as one record throw of 0 score.
In other words, if a Soldier throws two fault throws, those two throws count as one record throw.
Do not intentionally step over the line to increase your score. Doing so will result in 0 Score that is counted as one record throw.
Although Soldiers are required to execute two record throws and both record throws are recorded, only the longer of the two throws will count as the record score. The start line grader will circle the best score.
